Summary

»Our basic thesis is this: If your King is safely castled while the opponent’s is in the middle of the board, I want you to train your mind to view that central King as a target, and immediately try and ascertain whether there’s a way to smite it. At the very least, active play in the center while the enemy’s monarch resides there will allow you to make some important gains as he scrambles to get his King to safety.

»Other things being equal, the side with the vulnerable King will always come in second in a head to head clash.

»By now the reader should know the drill:

•Seeing that the enemy King has stayed in the center for too long, immediately get excited and target it for destruction;

•Don’t allow him to castle to safety;

•Bring as many pieces to the embattled area as possible and prepare some form of decisive penetration;

•Rip open some lines and go for the throat!
